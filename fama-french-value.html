Fama-French 'Value' (HML) Factor Replication

  • ROLE Computer Science Undergraduate
  • METHODS Factor Investing, Portfolio Construction, Time Series Analysis
  • TOOLS Python, Pandas, yfinance, Statsmodels, Matplotlib
  • REPOSITORY View on GitHub

1. The Objective

This project investigates one of the cornerstones of quantitative finance: the "value premium". The primary goal was to replicate the 'Value' (HML - High Minus Low) factor as defined by Eugene Fama and Kenneth French. The project involved sourcing and cleaning financial data, constructing value-based portfolios, and analyzing the historical performance of a long-short strategy.

2. Methodology: Portfolio Construction

The HML factor was constructed using the S&P 500 as the stock universe. The core methodology involved an annual portfolio rebalancing based on the Book-to-Market (B/M) ratio, the classic metric for "value".

The process, executed in a Python script, was as follows:

  • Data Sourcing: Acquired S&P 500 constituent lists and pulled annual 'bookValue' and 'marketCap' data for each company using the yfinance library.
  • Portfolio Sorting: At the start of each year, all stocks were sorted by their B/M ratio. They were then split into three portfolios: 'High' (top 30% B/M), 'Medium' (middle 40%), and 'Low' (bottom 30% B/M).
  • Factor Calculation: The monthly returns for the 'High' and 'Low' (equal-weighted) portfolios were calculated. The HML factor is the return of the zero-investment portfolio: Return(High) - Return(Low).

3. Analysis: Factor Performance

The analysis focused on validating the historical persistence of the value premium. The cumulative returns of the HML factor were plotted to provide a visual test of its long-term performance.

Key performance metrics were calculated, including the factor's annualized mean return, volatility, and Sharpe ratio. Finally, a Statsmodels OLS regression was used to measure the factor's statistical significance (alpha) and its beta exposure to the overall market, confirming its role as a distinct risk factor.
